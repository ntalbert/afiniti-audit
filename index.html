What's Working

Building Blocks for Growth

Before critiquing gaps, recognize what's strong. These aren't compliments — they're the foundation a marketing rebuild will leverage.

01

Partnership Momentum

Three marketplace launches in 9 months (AWS, Five9, NICE CXone). Multi-platform distribution away from Avaya dependence. Each partnership = co-marketing opportunity, backlink authority, and platform validation.

02

Proof Points That Competitors Can't Match

Named C-suite testimonials from AT&T, Virgin Media O2, TIM SpA, Accenture. T-Mobile: $70M incremental annual revenue from 50M optimized calls. No competitor has this depth of named, quantified enterprise validation.

03

Patented Differentiation

On/off benchmarking methodology is genuinely unique — no competitor offers controlled, continuous measurement of AI impact. "Outcome orchestration" positioning could define a category if supported by content infrastructure.

04

LinkedIn Base

265K followers — larger than Observe.AI, Cresta, and Balto combined. Legacy audience, but a distribution channel that exists and can be reactivated.

05

Wikipedia Structural Advantage

Afiniti has a Wikipedia page. Cresta, Observe.AI, and Balto do not. Wikipedia drives 47.9% of ChatGPT's top-10 citation share. This is a durable differentiator — once updated.

Action Plan

The 12-Month Rebuild

Phased implementation roadmap. Foundation first, then build, then scale.

Phase 1: Foundation

Weeks 1-8 — Stop the bleeding. Fix what's broken before building anything new.

Action Timeline Impact
Fix technical SEO: robots.txt, sitemap, schema markup, meta descriptions Weeks 1-2 Enables all future SEO
Resolve /resources/ 410 error Week 1 Recovers broken content hub
Unify brand architecture: one product story, one navigation Weeks 2-4 Eliminates buyer confusion
Create and submit G2 and Capterra profiles Week 2 Establishes review platform presence
Launch customer review generation campaign (target: 25-30 reviews) Weeks 2-8 Builds credibility baseline
Draft Wikipedia update with neutral, well-sourced content Weeks 3-6 Highest-leverage single asset
Build "What is Outcome Orchestration?" pillar page Weeks 4-6 Category ownership foundation
Create 3 standalone case studies (T-Mobile, Virgin Media O2, AT&T) Weeks 4-8 Mid-funnel sales assets + AEO citations

Phase 2: Build

Months 3-6 — Create the content engine and distribution infrastructure that doesn't exist today.

Action Timeline Impact
Launch 2x/week blog publishing cadence targeting keyword gaps Month 3+ Organic visibility foundation
Build interactive ROI calculator Month 3 Lead capture + sales enablement
Create 5 competitor comparison pages (Balto playbook) Months 3-4 High-intent search traffic capture
Launch YouTube channel: behavioral pairing explainers, customer stories Month 3 AEO citation source (6-19% of top-10)
Initiate Reddit community participation (educational, not promotional) Month 3+ Reddit = #1 AI citation source
Begin analyst relations program: brief Gartner, Forrester, IDC Month 4 6-12 month path to inclusion
Execute partner co-marketing: joint webinars with Five9, NICE, AWS Months 4-6 Backlinks + credibility transfer
Publish first original research report using anonymized proprietary data Months 5-6 Highest-impact content type

Phase 3: Scale

Months 7-12 — Compound the foundation. Shift from building to optimizing.

Action Timeline Impact
Establish AI Share of Voice measurement baseline and monthly tracking Month 7 Measure AEO progress vs. competitors
Launch gated content library (minimum 5 assets per industry vertical) Months 7-9 Mid-funnel nurture capability
Pursue Gartner/Forrester inclusion for adjacent reports Months 7-12 Analyst-driven buyer influence
Scale review generation to 100+ G2 reviews Months 7-12 Competitive parity with Balto
Launch customer advisory board Month 8 Community + content + retention
Evaluate paid search/SEM investment against organic traction Month 9 Data-driven paid amplification
Consider owned event around "Outcome Orchestration" category Months 10-12 Category authority + pipeline
